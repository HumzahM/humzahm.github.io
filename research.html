Conference Papers

  • Divergence Decoding: Targeted Unlearning via Auxiliary Models - Humzah Merchant, Bradford Levy
    International Conference on Machine Learning (ICML) 2026 (Poster | Paper)
    Motivated by earlier work on addressing look-ahead bias—which is a special case of unlearning—applied a method of debiasing logits more broadly to unlearning benchmarks and found that it is state of the art across most of them.
  • Towards Financial World Modeling - Humzah Merchant, Alec Guthrie, Simon Mahns, Randall Balestriero, Bradford Levy
    Preprint
    Exploring the current state of SSL on financial markets, we find that while supervised learning beats SSL on many core tasks, representations from SSL models generalize better to auxiliary downstream tasks.

Workshop Papers

  • A Fast and Effective Solution to the Problem of Look-ahead Bias in LLMs - Humzah Merchant, Bradford Levy
    Best Poster Award at NeurIPS 2025 Workshop: Generative AI in Finance (Poster | Paper)
    Drew on product of experts and importance sampling to develop a method for reducing and/or eliminating look-ahead bias in LLMs. Demonstrated its efficacy across a variety of finance-specific tasks.
  • Forecasting With LLMs: Improved Generalization Through Feature Steering - Humzah Merchant, Bradford Levy
    ICML 2026 Workshop: Forecasting as a New Frontier of Intelligence (Poster)
    Amplifying features associated with time-awareness in LLMs can reduce reliance on information from after a cutoff specified at inference-time.
  • Optimizing Pre-Training of Tabular Foundation Models by Shaping Geometry - Humzah Merchant, Sriniketh Vangaru, Randall Balestriero
    ICML 2026 Workshop: Foundation Models for Structured Data (Poster)
    TFMs motivate well-distributed intermediate embeddings; although supervised pretraining does not naturally produce them, explicitly shaping early-layer geometry improves classification performance.
